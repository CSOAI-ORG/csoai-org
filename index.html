The CSOAI Charter

52 articles establishing the governance framework for the global AI economy

The CSOAI Charter represents the foundational legal and technical framework enabling global AI governance. Structured across 6 implementation phases, it establishes baseline safety standards, certification requirements, and regulatory harmonization pathways across 40+ allied nations.
Phase 1: Foundation & Authority (Articles 1-8) +
Establishes CSOAI as the international governance authority for artificial intelligence. Defines organizational structure, the Founding Council of 24 members across 9 countries, and basic principles for all subsequent governance. Creates the legal foundation for all member-state participation and mutual recognition frameworks.
Phase 2: Safety & Standards (Articles 9-18) +
Defines minimum safety standards for AI systems. Requires security testing through AIdome, alignment verification through Anthropic methodologies, and transparency requirements. Establishes baseline requirements that all AI systems must meet for network participation.
Phase 3: Certification & Testing (Articles 19-28) +
Establishes the CASA certification program with three levels: Commercial, Government, and Defence/NATO. Defines testing protocols, auditor qualifications, and certification renewal requirements. Creates economic incentives for certified systems through Proof of AI.
Phase 4: Distribution & Network (Articles 29-38) +
Operationalizes the 40-nation network through DSRB and CSOAI distribution channels. Establishes mutual recognition protocols ensuring a single certification enables compliance across borders. Creates international arbitration mechanisms for disputes.
Phase 5: Workforce & Intelligence (Articles 39-46) +
Establishes the AI Safety Auditor certification pipeline through BMCC-CUNY and Digital Human Library. Targets 40,000+ trained professionals. Creates intelligence-sharing protocols between national security agencies. Integrates with defence and intelligence infrastructure.
Phase 6: Compliance & Enforcement (Articles 47-52) +
Establishes enforcement mechanisms, penalty structures, and compliance verification. Creates mechanisms for revoking certifications. Defines periodic reassessment requirements. Establishes grievance procedures and appeal pathways. Ensures global standards are maintained as AI capabilities advance.

CASA Certification Program

Three-tier certification for AI systems from commercial to defence-critical applications

The Certified AI Safety Auditor (CASA) program establishes rigorous, three-tier certification levels for AI systems. Each level includes comprehensive security testing, alignment verification, and ongoing compliance monitoring tailored to the operational context and risk profile.
Level 1
Commercial
$5K - $25K
  • AIdome basic testing
  • Safety alignment verification
  • Transparency compliance
  • Annual reassessment
  • Standard incident reporting
  • Public certification
Level 2
Government

  • Government compliance
