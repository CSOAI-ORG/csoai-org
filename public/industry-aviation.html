Industry Overview

Aviation is uniquely positioned at the intersection of AI innovation and the most stringent safety regulation in any industry. AI systems now assist with flight planning, predictive engine maintenance, air traffic management sequencing, crew scheduling, baggage handling, and passenger biometric processing. The sector's adoption of computer vision for runway inspection, natural language processing for air-ground communications, and machine learning for fuel optimisation creates a dense compliance environment where every model must satisfy aviation safety authorities (EASA, FAA, CASA) alongside emerging AI regulations. The EU AI Act classifies many aviation AI use cases as high-risk — particularly those affecting the safety of aircraft operations, pilot decision-support, and passenger processing — triggering mandatory conformity assessments, human oversight requirements, and continuous monitoring obligations.

🇪🇺 EU AI Act Relevance

Aviation AI systems that influence flight safety, crew workload, or passenger rights fall under Article 6 (high-risk classification) and Annex III categories 1 (biometrics), 5 (employment/crew management), and 7 (transport safety). Mandatory requirements include risk management (Art 9), technical documentation (Art 11), record-keeping (Art 12), transparency (Art 13), human oversight (Art 14), and accuracy/robustness (Art 15). Systems affecting aircraft airworthiness may additionally require Article 43 conformity assessment with a Notified Body. The Article 50 transparency obligations (2 Aug 2026 cliff) apply to all AI-generated aviation documentation, maintenance logs, and passenger communications.

🛡 CASA Certification Pathway

The CSOAI Aviation Certification Pathway follows a five-stage process: Stage 1 — Gap Analysis (mapping AI systems to EASA AI Roadmap Level 1/2 and EU AI Act obligations); Stage 2 — Risk Assessment (classification under Annex III with aviation-specific hazard analysis); Stage 3 — Technical Documentation (model card, training data provenance, safety case, and human-machine interface report); Stage 4 — Conformity Assessment (third-party audit by CSOAI-accredited assessors with Notified Body coordination); Stage 5 — Continuous Monitoring (quarterly re-assessment, incident reporting, and model update governance). Target timeline: 6–8 weeks for initial certification. CASA certification is recognised under CSOAI's mutual recognition framework with EASA and FAA.
