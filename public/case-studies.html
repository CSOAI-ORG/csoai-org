Case 1: EU healthcare AI diagnostic (anonymised)

Hospitals · 3 EU member states · 12M patients · Article 50 high-risk (Annex III #1 health)
The scenario A medical AI vendor sells a chest X-ray triage system to 40 hospitals across Germany, France, and Italy. The AI is "intended to be used as a second-reader" — meaning a radiologist confirms every diagnosis. But the AI's risk profile is high: it's used in critical care, with potential for harm if it produces false negatives.

The compliance gap The vendor's existing compliance documentation: 47 pages in 4 languages. No single verify URL a radiologist or hospital can check. The vendor's auditor wants Article 9 (risk management), Article 10 (data governance), Article 11 (technical docs), Article 14 (human oversight) — all four have to be checkable in 1 click.

Each cert is Ed25519-signed, hash-chained into the sigil bus, and references the EU AI Act, MDR, HIPAA, ISO 13485, and the hospital's specific configuration. The cert URL is public — the radiologist, the auditor, the hospital's CISO can all check it in 1 click.

Common patterns

All 3 cases share these patterns:

  • Article 50 high-risk classification (Annex III #1 health, #5 credit, #5 essential services)
  • Multi-jurisdiction compliance (3-5 regulators per AI system)
  • Pre-existing compliance documentation that's difficult to verify in 1 click
  • Need for public verify URL (regulator + customer + auditor access)
  • White-label opportunity (the customer's branding on the cert)

What csoai.org would NOT do

To be clear about scope:

  • We don't replace your existing risk management process — we certify its output
  • We don't replace your legal counsel — we provide the signed evidence they can use
  • We don't replace your auditor — we make their job 10x faster
  • We don't claim to make you "compliant" — only your regulator can decide that
  • We don't lock you in — every cert is yours forever, with the Ed25519 keys published
