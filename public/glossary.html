EU AI Act (35 terms)

AI Act

Regulation (EU) 2024/1689. Lays down harmonised rules on AI. Entered into force 1 Aug 2024.

Annex III

The 8 high-risk AI use cases (biometrics, critical infrastructure, education, employment, essential services, law enforcement, migration, justice/democracy).

Article 5

Prohibited AI practices (social scoring, manipulative AI, real-time biometric ID in public spaces).

Article 6

High-risk classification. AI is high-risk if it's a safety component of a product covered by EU harmonisation legislation, OR listed in Annex III.

Article 9

Risk management system. Continuous, iterative process run throughout the high-risk AI lifecycle.

Article 10

Data and data governance. Training/validation/test datasets must be relevant, representative, and free of errors.

Article 11

Technical documentation. Mandatory Annex IV documentation proving compliance.

Article 12

Record-keeping. Automatic logging of events over the high-risk AI lifecycle.

Article 13

Transparency and provision of information to deployers. Users must understand the AI's capabilities and limitations.

Article 14

Human oversight. Designed to allow natural persons to oversee, intervene, or shut down the AI.

Article 15

Accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity. AI must be resilient to errors, faults, and adversarial attacks.

Article 16

Obligations of providers of high-risk AI systems.

Article 17

Quality management system for providers.

Article 26

Obligations of deployers of high-risk AI systems.

Article 27

Fundamental rights impact assessment (FRIA) for high-risk AI in public sector.

Article 43

Conformity assessment. Third-party assessment by notified body for some Annex III high-risk systems.

Article 47

EU declaration of conformity.

Article 49

Registration. High-risk AI systems must be registered in the EU database before market placement.

Article 50

Transparency obligations for providers and deployers of certain AI systems. Disclosure + labelling requirements.

Article 51

Classification rules for general-purpose AI (GPAI) models.

Article 52

Obligations for providers of GPAI models. Technical documentation + downstream provider cooperation.

Article 53

Obligations for providers of GPAI models with systemic risk.

Article 55

Obligations for deployers of GPAI systems with systemic risk.

Article 70

AI Pact. Voluntary framework for early compliance.

AI Office

European AI Office (DG CNECT). Implements the AI Act at the EU level.

AI Board

European Artificial Intelligence Board. Member state representatives coordinating AI Act implementation.

Scientific Panel

Independent scientific experts advising the AI Office on GPAI models and emerging risks.

Notified Body

Conformity assessment body designated by an EU member state.

Conformity Assessment

Process demonstrating whether an AI system meets AI Act requirements (Article 43).

CE Marking

Conformité Européenne. Mandatory marking for products in the EU market, including high-risk AI.

GPAI

General-Purpose AI model. AI model with significant generality (Article 3 #63).

Systemic Risk

Risk from a GPAI model with high-impact capabilities. Triggers Article 55 obligations.

Deployer

Natural or legal person using an AI system under their authority (Article 3 #4).

Provider

Developer of the AI system or GPAI model, or the entity placing it on the market (Article 3 #3).

Distributor / Importer

Other economic operators in the AI value chain (Articles 24, 25).

White House Office of Science & Tech Policy 2022 framework. Non-binding.

Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework: Generative AI Profile.

OMB Memorandum on Advancing the Responsible Acquisition of AI for the Federal Government.

California Safe and Secure Innovation for Frontier AI Act (vetoed 2024).

Utah AI Policy Act (2024). Disclosure + disclosure correction.

CFPB AI

CFPB guidance on adverse action notices for AI-driven credit decisions.

FTC enforcement actions on AI deception, bias, and unfairness (Section 5).

EEOC AI

EEOC guidance on AI-driven employment discrimination (Title VII).

FDA Software as a Medical Device with AI/ML. Pre-market + post-market requirements.

General Compliance (25 terms)

Watchdog Cert

CSOAI's per-AI-system signed attestation. Ed25519, public verify URL.

Ed25519

EdDSA signature algorithm using Curve25519. RFC 8032.

Sigil

CSOAI's term for a signed record in the chain. Hash-linked.

Sigil Bus

CSOAI's append-only log of Ed25519-signed records. Audit trail.

Keystone Cert

CSOAI's free, 30-second signed attestation. Lead magnet.

Conformity

Meeting the requirements of a regulation.

Certification

Third-party attestation of conformity.

Attestation

Statement of conformity. Can be self-attested or third-party.

Audit Trail

Chronological record of system activity. Tamper-evident.

Byzantine Fault Tolerance

Distributed consensus algorithm that tolerates malicious nodes. BFT.

BFT Council

CSOAI's 200-voter council for AI decision reconciliation.

PDCA

Plan-Do-Check-Act. Continuous improvement cycle (Deming).

FRIA

Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (Article 27).

DPIA

Data Protection Impact Assessment (GDPR Article 35).

Algorithmic Impact Assessment

AIA. Risk assessment for algorithmic decision systems.

Risk Classification

Categorizing AI system risk (prohibited, high-risk, limited, minimal).

Risk Management

Systematic process for identifying, assessing, mitigating AI risks.

Bias Audit

Statistical + qualitative review for disparate impact.

Model Card

Documented model metadata: training data, intended use, performance.

Datasheet for Datasets

Gebru et al. 2021. Documented dataset provenance.

Sheets of AI Safety

MIT/Stanford/CMU. Model deployment + maintenance documentation.

Red Teaming

Adversarial testing of AI for safety, bias, and security issues.

Adversarial Example

Input crafted to fool an AI model. FGSM, PGD, etc.

Membership Inference

Attack that determines if a data point was in the training set.

Model Inversion

Attack that reconstructs training data from a model.

Technical (25 terms)

MCP

Model Context Protocol. Open protocol for tool-using AI.

JSON-RPC

JSON-RPC 2.0. Lightweight RPC protocol used by MCP.

LLM

Large Language Model. GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, Llama, etc.

RLHF

Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback.

RAG

Retrieval-Augmented Generation. Grounding LLMs with retrieval.

Vector DB

Vector database (Pinecone, Weaviate, Qdrant, pgvector).

Embeddings

Vector representations of text, images, or other data.

Token

Sub-word unit of LLM input/output.

Context Window

Max tokens an LLM can process at once. 8K → 1M+.

Fine-tuning

Adapting a pre-trained model to a specific task.

LoRA

Low-Rank Adaptation. Parameter-efficient fine-tuning.

QLoRA

Quantized LoRA. Lower VRAM for fine-tuning.

Distillation

Training a smaller model to mimic a larger one.

Quantization

Reducing model precision (FP32 → INT8, etc.).

Prompt Engineering

Crafting inputs to elicit desired LLM behavior.

Chain-of-Thought

Prompting technique: ask the model to think step-by-step.

Function Calling

LLM invokes external tools via structured output.

Agent

AI system that autonomously takes actions toward goals.

Multi-Agent

Multiple agents collaborating or competing.

Tool Use

LLM invokes APIs, databases, code execution, etc.

ReAct

Reasoning + Acting. Agent loop pattern (Yao et al. 2022).

Reflexion

Self-reflection + memory for agent improvement (Shinn et al. 2023).

Constitutional AI

RLHF with self-critique against a set of principles.

Spectre / Meltdown

Speculative execution vulnerabilities. Relevant to AI hardware security.

SHA-256

SHA-256 hash. Used in CSOAI's sigil chain.

CSOAI's care-oriented LLM layer. Adjacent to the action LLM.

Consciousness

CSOAI's 0.787 metric. Reflection cycles × dreams × memory replay.

Dream

Episode

CSOAI's coherent experience unit. Multiple memos.

On-chain

Off-chain
