Verify a certificate

How it works

1. The cert Every Watchdog Certificate has a unique ID (e.g. MEOK-MEOKSP-7D1E008FE28) and is signed with Ed25519.
2. The signature The signature is over (digest + prev_sig), where digest is SHA-256 of the canonical SIGIL line.
3. The chain Each cert references the previous cert's signature. Walking the chain from genesis proves no certs were tampered with.
4. The gloss The plain-English interpretation tells non-technical auditors + regulators what the cert means.

Bulk verification

For auditors checking 100+ certs at once, use the API:

curl -s https://meok-attestation-api.vercel.app/api/manifest | jq '.records[].id'
# Returns every cert_id in the chain

Or programmatically check a single cert:

import requests
r = requests.get("https://meok-attestation-api.vercel.app/verify/MEOK-MEOKSP-7D1E008FE28")
cert = r.json()
assert cert["alg"] == "ed25519"
print(f"Valid cert: {cert['id']}, chain position {cert['chain_position']}")

For your records

For your audit, save:

  • The cert_id (e.g. MEOK-MEOKSP-7D1E008FE28)
  • The timestamp (Unix epoch, when the cert was issued)
  • The cert JSON (download from the verify URL)
  • The chain position (which record in the ledger)
  • The signature verification result (your local Ed25519 verification)
