Framework Crosswalk

EU AI Act ↔ NIST AI RMF ↔ ISO/IEC 42001. Three regimes, one control set. Map your obligations once, generate one evidence trail, and stop running parallel compliance programmes.

The three regimes

Law — EU

EU AI Act

Regulation (EU) 2024/1689. Binding, risk-based law with prohibited practices, high-risk obligations (Articles 9–15), and Article 50 transparency duties. Penalties up to €35m or 7% of turnover. Article 50 and Annex III apply 2 Aug 2026.

Framework — US

NIST AI RMF

Voluntary US framework (AI RMF 1.0). Organised around four functions — Govern, Map, Measure, Manage — to identify and treat AI risk across the lifecycle. Widely used to operationalise legal obligations.

Standard — ISO/IEC

ISO/IEC 42001

The first certifiable AI management-system standard. A Plan-Do-Check-Act backbone (clauses 4–10) plus Annex A controls. Provides the governance structure and audit trail regulators expect.

Obligation mapping

Each EU AI Act obligation maps to a NIST AI RMF function and an ISO/IEC 42001 clause that already produces the relevant evidence. Build the control once; satisfy all three.

How to use it

1. Anchor on ISO/IEC 42001. Stand up an AI management system as your governance backbone — policy, roles, risk treatment, continual improvement.

2. Operationalise with NIST AI RMF. Run the Govern / Map / Measure / Manage functions as the day-to-day activities your management system requires.

3. Bind to EU AI Act articles. Use the table above to attach each legal obligation to the function and clause that already generates the evidence.

4. Generate one evidence trail. Capture documentation once and reuse it across all three regimes for audits, conformity assessment, and certification.
