Government / Public Sector AI Compliance: UK AI Regulation

UK public-sector AI is governed by the Central Digital and Data Office (CDDO) AI guidance, Cabinet Office spend controls, and sector regulators. The pro-innovation approach emphasises responsible adoption through existing governance rather than a dedicated AI statute.

Sector: Government / Public Sector Regulation: UK AI Regulation CSOAI Articles: 4 mapped

Key Compliance Requirements

CDDO Algorithmic Transparency Recording Standard

Central government departments must publish algorithmic transparency records for AI used in decision-making, including purpose, data sources, and human oversight.

Cabinet Office spend controls for AI

High-risk or high-value AI procurements require approval through the Cabinet Office spend-control process, with scrutiny of ethics, data, and security.

Sector regulator guidance

Public-sector AI in health, policing, education, and transport must comply with guidance from the relevant sector regulator — MHRA, College of Policing, DfE, ORR, etc.

Data ethics framework

Government departments are expected to apply the UK Government Data Ethics Framework to AI projects, ensuring fairness, accountability, and transparency in public-service AI.
